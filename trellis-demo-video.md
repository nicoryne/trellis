# Trellis · 2-Minute Demo Video

**Type:** Recorded demo video (not live stage)
**Duration:** 2:00 (target 1:55–2:05; hard ceiling 2:10)
**Audience:** Hackathon judges, prospective firm leaders, anyone seeing Trellis for the first time
**Companion to:** [`trellis-demo-super-narrative.md`](trellis-demo-super-narrative.md) (the 5-min live stage script). This file is the compressed video adaptation.

---

## What This Video Must Accomplish

In 120 seconds, a viewer with zero context must walk away understanding three things:

1. **What Trellis is** — a privacy-first knowledge fabric for law firms.
2. **Why it can exist** when no other legal AI tool can — architecture, not policy. Privileged content never leaves the device in raw form.
3. **Why it matters strategically** — it's the substrate that makes every other legal AI tool firm-aware.

Everything in the script is in service of those three takeaways.

Total voiceover budget: ~270 words at 135 wpm. The script below is exactly 277 words.

## Recording Instructions

### Tooling

| Need | Recommended |
|---|---|
| Screen capture | **OBS Studio** (free, frame-perfect, 60fps capable) — or Loom / ScreenStudio if you want auto cursor smoothing |
| Audio | USB condenser mic (Blue Yeti, Shure MV7) recorded *separately* from screen — never trust laptop mic |
| Voiceover editing | **Audacity** (free) or Adobe Audition — noise gate + light compression + de-esser |
| Video editing | **DaVinci Resolve** (free, professional) — or CapCut / iMovie if time-pressed |
| Cursor enhancement | macOS: Mouseposé; Windows: built-in cursor highlight or Pinpoint; ScreenStudio bakes this in |

### Capture settings

- Resolution: **1920×1080** at **60fps** (the graph overlay animation needs smoothness)
- Codec: H.264, high bitrate (20+ Mbps for the master file)
- Browser zoom: **100% exactly** — verify before every take
- Browser chrome: full-screen Chrome with one tab only; hide bookmarks bar (Cmd/Ctrl+Shift+B)
- OS UI: hide dock/taskbar, enable Do Not Disturb, silence everything
- Cursor: enable cursor highlight in your screen recorder so viewers can track movement

### Voiceover guidelines

- **Record voiceover separately** from screen capture. Sync in post.
- Record each beat as its own take. Don't try to perform 2 minutes in one shot.
- Pace target: **130–140 words per minute**. Slower than conversation. Faster than a lecture.
- Energy: confident, not breathless. You are explaining a serious product to serious people.
- Smile slightly on Beat 1 and Beat 5 — it warms the tone. Drop the smile for Beats 3 and 4 (these are the substance beats).
- After every take, listen back at 1.0x speed. If you wouldn't trust the lawyer on screen, redo it.
- Record 2–3 takes per beat minimum. The "good enough" take is usually take 3.

### Screen capture takes

- **Capture each beat in its own recording session.** Splice in editing.
- Beats 2 and 4 are the trickiest — Beat 2 because entity extraction timing varies; Beat 4 because the overlay must be pristine. Plan for 5+ takes each.
- For Beat 4, the overlay animation is the hero. If it stutters, recapture. If you can pre-warm the model so streaming starts quickly, do it.
- For Beat 3, the cursor pathing across redactions is the cinematography. Practice it. Slow, deliberate, never twitchy.

### Editing notes

- Cuts between beats should be hard (no fades) — fades read as TV, hard cuts read as software.
- **Crossfade audio** at every cut. Hard audio cuts sound amateur even when video cuts are clean.
- Add subtle **background music** only at very low level (-30dB). Suggestions: Tom Misch–style minimal piano, or a single sustained synth pad. No drums, no melody that competes with the voiceover. Music must duck out during Beat 4's 4-second silence.
- Add **captions** for the entire video. Many viewers will watch with sound off. Caption font: Inter or system sans, 28pt, bottom third, with a 60% black backdrop pill.
- Beat 3's redaction modal needs **zoom emphasis**. Push in slightly (5–10%) as the cursor walks the redactions. Pull back for the Publish click.
- Beat 4's query overlay must be shown at native resolution — do not crop it. The whole-screen visual is the point.

## Pre-Recording Checklist

Run this 30 minutes before your first take. Every item, every time.

### Trellis state

- [ ] Trellis running locally or on staging URL, whichever has the freshest state
- [ ] Seed data freshly loaded (`POST /api/seed` or run `team-graph.seed.ts`)
- [ ] All three demo accounts verified working:
  - `litigator@acme.law` / `demo`
  - `lead@acme.law` / `demo`
  - `admin@acme.law` / `demo`
- [ ] Mandatory demo query (`What has our firm learned about cross-examining expert witnesses on damages calculations?`) tested twice — confirms `confidence: high`, 5+ citations, 3+ authors, 2+ matters
- [ ] Microphone permission granted in browser
- [ ] Gemini API key has remaining quota
- [ ] Postgres + Presidio + Whisper all healthy

### Environment

- [ ] Demo laptop plugged in, fan ramped down (a hot machine drops frames)
- [ ] All notifications silenced; Do Not Disturb on
- [ ] Single Chrome window, single tab, no extensions visible
- [ ] Bookmarks bar hidden
- [ ] System dock / taskbar hidden
- [ ] Browser zoom = 100%
- [ ] Display resolution = 1920×1080 (or downscale in post)
- [ ] Cursor highlight enabled in OBS / recorder

### Voiceover setup

- [ ] Mic positioned 6–8 inches from mouth, slightly off-axis to reduce plosives
- [ ] Pop filter in place
- [ ] Room quiet — no HVAC, no fans, no laptop fan audible
- [ ] Audacity / Audition recording at 48kHz, 24-bit, mono
- [ ] Test recording done — listen back, check for room noise, sibilance, clipping
- [ ] Glass of water within reach (room temperature, not cold — cold tightens vocal cords)

### Continuity

- [ ] Same Trellis state across all takes — if you re-seed mid-recording, the personal graph will look different and break visual continuity
- [ ] Same browser window dimensions across all takes
- [ ] Same time-of-day if possible — natural light from a window can shift between morning and afternoon takes and produce subtle color differences

## Anti-Patterns: What Not to Do

These are the most common ways video demos fail not from technical issues but from creative decisions.

### Don't show your cursor flying around

A frantic cursor reads as a panicked operator. Move slowly. Pause before every click. The viewer's eye needs time to follow.

### Don't oversell with music

Tempting to layer dramatic synth swells under Beat 3. Resist. The redaction visual carries itself. Music at -30dB, ducked entirely during Beat 4's 4-second silence.

### Don't apologize for fictional data

Acme Litigation Partners and Dr. Janet Mei are treated as real throughout the video. No "this is seed data" disclaimers, no asterisks, no captions undermining the narrative.

### Don't include a face-cam overlay

This is a product demo, not a vlog. The screen is the hero. If you must humanize, add a single 2-second presenter intro frame at the very start — but the rest of the video is the product.

### Don't add stock B-roll

No suited lawyers nodding in slow motion. No glowing AI brain animations. No abstract circuit boards. The Trellis UI is the story.

### Don't go over 2:00

A 2:00 video that lands beats Beat 5 cleanly is dramatically better than a 2:25 video that fits more product. The viewer's attention has a hard cliff at the two-minute mark. Cut content, never sacrifice the close.

### Don't skip captions

40–60% of online viewers watch with sound off by default. If your captions are missing or auto-generated and wrong, half your audience never hears the script.

## Variants

### 60-second cut (for socials / Twitter / LinkedIn)

Drop Beat 1 entirely. Open cold on the personal graph.

| Beat | Time | Compression |
|---|---|---|
| Capture | 0:00–0:10 | Cut to just the transcript-and-entities reveal — skip the audio recording |
| Redaction | 0:10–0:35 | Keep nearly full weight; this is the hero |
| Query overlay | 0:35–0:55 | Cut the citation click; let the overlay carry it |
| Close | 0:55–1:00 | "Trellis — the privacy-first knowledge fabric for law firms. The layer that makes legal AI actually work." |

### 30-second cut (for paid ads)

Single line voiceover. Pure visual:

> "Lawyers can't use AI on privileged work — unless privacy is the architecture. Trellis."

Opens on the redaction modal. Holds for 8 seconds while the diff is visible. Cuts to the query overlay (8 seconds). Cuts to the closing wordmark.

### Captionless / silent cut (for muted autoplay)

Same video, but with **on-screen text callouts** replacing the voiceover at every beat. Text appears in the lower third in the Inter typeface, animated in with a 200ms fade. The redaction and overlay visuals are designed to read silent — leverage that.

## Final Note

A 2-minute video lives or dies in three moments: the **first 5 seconds**, the **redaction reveal**, and the **graph overlay**. The rest is connective tissue.

Spend disproportionate editing effort on those three. Re-cut Beat 1 until the hook lands in 5 seconds flat. Re-cut Beat 3 until the redactions are unmistakable. Re-cut Beat 4 until the overlay animation is frame-perfect.

Everything else can be merely good. Those three have to be great.
