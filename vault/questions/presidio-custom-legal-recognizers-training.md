# V1 fine-tuned legal recognizer — training data and provenance?

## Status

**Open** — awaiting Keith's answer.

## Why it matters

V1's four-pass [[redaction-pipeline]] adds **Pass 1: privilege detection (fine-tuned legal model + [[microsoft-presidio|Presidio]])**. The fine-tuned model is the credibility hinge for the privacy story — but no training corpus, no labeled-data source, and no validation methodology are documented. A poorly-trained privilege detector that leaks once is a customer-canceling incident. (see [[microsoft-presidio]], [[redaction-pipeline]])

## What we know so far

- MVP uses Presidio out-of-the-box (PII regex + NER) for Pass 1.
- V1 promises a fine-tuned legal recognizer layered on top.
- No training data, model architecture, or eval set named.

## What would resolve it

- Pick a base model (a fine-tuned small open model, a custom-trained classifier, a Gemini few-shot prompt approach).
- Source labeled training data: synthetic generated by Gemini? Licensed legal corpora? Customer-firm seed data with consent?
- Define an eval set: precision/recall targets on privileged-vs-non-privileged passages.
