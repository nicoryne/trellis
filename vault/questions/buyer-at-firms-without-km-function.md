# Who is the buyer at firms without a dedicated KM function?

## Status

**Open** — awaiting Keith's answer.

## Why it matters

The [[trellis-business-model|business model]] names the [[knowledge-admin|KM Partner / KM Director]] as the primary buyer. The brief acknowledges that "the bottom end of SAM outsources KM to consultants" — meaning the named buyer does not exist at a meaningful slice of the addressable market. Without a defined secondary buyer, those firms are unreachable through the primary motion. (see [[knowledge-admin]])

## What we know so far

- Primary buyer: KM Partner / KM Director.
- Secondary influencer: [[practice-group-lead|Litigation Practice Group Lead]].
- Long-tail firms: outsource KM to consultants → no KM Partner exists internally.
- Brief mentions "partnership channels needed" without naming partners.

## What would resolve it

- Define the substitute buyer at no-KM-function firms (likely the Managing Partner directly, or the Litigation PG Lead acting as champion).
- Name candidate channel partners (legal-KM consulting firms: Bigfork, Helm360, Wilson Allen Corporation, HBR Consulting).
- Decide whether channel-partner sales is V1 or V2.
