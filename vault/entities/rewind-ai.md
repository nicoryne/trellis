# Rewind.ai

A consumer-facing always-on capture product (screen + audio) marketed as "personal AI that remembers everything." In the [[trellis|Trellis]] decision history, Rewind serves as a **rejected reference** — the concrete reason why the team did not pursue screen-capture-style ingestion for an enterprise product.

## Key facts

- **What it does**: continuous screen capture + audio capture + AI indexing across the user's day. (see [[trellis-context-dump]])
- **Commercial signal**: "struggling with this exact model. Adoption mixed despite huge funding."
- **Why rejected for Trellis** (per Phase 5 of the [[trellis-context-dump|context dump]]):
  1. Enterprise procurement blocker — consulting firms (McKinsey, Bain, etc.) won't allow software that screenshots client work; compliance risk too high.
  2. "One-button capture" doesn't reduce friction — the user still has to decide what's worth capturing.
  3. Audio capture has even bigger legal problems — two-party consent states, fireable offenses at most professional services firms.
  4. Local-first claim has limits — if AI processing happens in the cloud, the privacy pitch collapses.
- **The deeper reframe** that came out of considering Rewind: *"The problem isn't 'how do we make capture more convenient.' The problem is 'people don't capture useful knowledge.' Easier capture doesn't solve knowledge sharing — it just creates more noise."*
